San Francisco, CA · Series B · 101–250 employees · LinkedIn · Website

Its pole-mounted Gridscope sensors monitor the physical, electrical, and environmental conditions of power infrastructure in real time, detecting hazards like fallen limbs, arcing, and mechanical failures before they cause outages or wildfires. The platform powers what the company calls Active Grid Response: immediate detection, precise fault localization, and continuous monitoring even during outages. Utility partners collectively serve roughly 40% of electricity customers in the US. One partner, Duquesne Light, earned a national innovation award after deploying 1,700 additional Gridscopes following its initial rollout. Gridware reported 7x year-over-year revenue growth through mid-2025 and reached cash flow positivity before its Series A. The company raised $55M Series B in November 2025 led by Tiger Global and Generation Investment Management, with continued backing from Sequoia Capital and Y Combinator, and is now expanding into the UK and continental Europe.

Who should reach out: B2B sales consultants, demand gen agencies, and content strategists with energy, utilities, or infrastructure experience.
